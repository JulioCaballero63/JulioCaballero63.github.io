White Space

Apple

Apple.com

WhiteSpace is the area that separates elements in a webpage. White space does not necessarily have to be white, it can be any color, texture, pattern, or even a background image. White space sometime causes frustration between designers and clients. Design principles encourage the use of white space to help pages look better and allow for a better user experience. It is common for some clients to consider white space wasted space. They believe all that empty space should be used to display more information or other visual elements. But in reality, white space allows us to accomplish balance in our design elements, keep the content organized and have a better visual experience. People loose interest when all they see are line after line of information or a webpage crowded with pictures, it also makes it difficult to navigate. Apple.com is a good example of the white space design principle being used on a webpage design.
