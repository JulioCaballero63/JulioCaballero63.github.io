White Space

Apple

Apple.com

White Space is the area that separates elements in a webpage. White space does not necessarily have to be white, it can be any color, texture, pattern, or even a background image. White space sometime causes frustration between designers and clients. Design principles encourage the use of white space to help pages look better and allow for a better user experience. It is common for some clients to consider white space wasted space. They believe all that empty space should be used to display more information or other visual elements. But in reality, white space allows us to accomplish balance in our design elements, keep the content organized and have a better visual experience. People loose interest when all they see are line after line of information or a webpage crowded with pictures, it also makes it difficult to navigate. Apple.com is a good example of the white space design principle being used on a webpage design.

Proximity

Amazon

Amazon.com

Proximity is the grouping of text or images in a way that it suggests that those items are related or not related to each other. We should visually group similar or related items together to emphasize their relationship. Also we should separate unrelated items to emphasize their lack of relationship. Amazon.com is a good example of the proximity principle, the web site groups related items and makes it easier to shop or compare items.

Alignment

Idaho Power

Idahopower.com

Alignment is the principle that suggest that elements should be place in a certain way so that the elements are easier to read. Alignment is used to organize or group items, it is also used to create balance, structure, and connections between items. Idaho Power's website is a good example of how we can use alignment to organize a web page. All of the text on the page is aligned to the left.
